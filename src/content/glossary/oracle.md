[Oracle Database](https://www.oracle.com/database/) is a commercial relational database management system ([[relational-database|RDBMS]]) and one of the most influential pieces of software ever shipped: released in 1979, it was the **first commercial [[sql|SQL]] database**, beating IBM — whose own research invented the relational model and SQL — to market.

## Features & Architecture

Oracle is a heavyweight client-server system aimed squarely at large enterprises. It offers an enormous feature set: sophisticated [[transaction|transaction]] management and multi-version [[concurrency|concurrency]] control (which it pioneered commercially), partitioning for very large [[table|tables]], Real Application Clusters (RAC) for running one database across several machines, fine-grained [[access-control|security controls]], and its own procedural language, **PL/SQL**, for writing stored programs inside the database. This power comes with matching complexity and licensing costs, which is why smaller projects usually reach for [[postgresql|PostgreSQL]] or [[mysql|MySQL]] instead.

## History

Larry Ellison, Bob Miner, and Ed Oates founded the company (originally Software Development Laboratories) after reading IBM's [[system-r|System R]] papers, and shipped Oracle V2 in 1979. Through the 1980s and 90s Oracle became *the* database of big business, and the company grew into one of the largest software vendors in the world. Banks, airlines, telecoms, and governments still run enormous Oracle installations today.
