---
term: Schema
short: The structure of a database — its tables, their columns and types, and the rules on them.
---

A database's schema is its shape: which [[table|tables]] exist, what
[[column|columns]] and [[data-type|types]] each has, and the constraints and
[[relationship|relationships]] between them.

Relational databases expect a schema up front, which is what lets them enforce
rules. Some [[nosql]] databases are "schema-less", letting each record vary in
shape — flexibility that trades away those guarantees.
