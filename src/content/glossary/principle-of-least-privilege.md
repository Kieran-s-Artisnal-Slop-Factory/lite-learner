---
term: Principle of least privilege
short: Every account gets the minimum access it needs to do its job — limiting mistakes and breaches.
---

Least privilege means each person or program is granted only the powers its job
requires. A reporting dashboard that can only read public data is an annoyance if
compromised, not a catastrophe.

It's the core idea behind database [[access-control]]: minimize what each account
can do, and both honest mistakes and break-ins stay contained.
