[Db2](https://www.ibm.com/db2) is IBM's flagship relational database management system ([[relational-database|RDBMS]]), released in 1983 as the direct commercial descendant of the [[system-r|System R]] research project — the project that proved the relational model could perform and invented [[sql|SQL]].

## Features & Architecture

Db2 is a server-based system built for large enterprise and mainframe workloads. It runs on IBM Z mainframes as well as Linux, Unix, and Windows, and offers deep [[transaction|transaction]] processing strength, advanced [[query-planner|query optimization]] (an area IBM Research has led for decades), row- and column-organized storage for mixed operational and analytical work, and tight integration with IBM's mainframe ecosystem, where it often runs alongside the much older hierarchical [[ims|IMS]].

## History

After Edgar Codd published the relational model in 1970, IBM was slow to commercialize it — its hierarchical [[ims|IMS]] was hugely profitable — which let [[oracle|Oracle]] ship the first commercial SQL database in 1979. IBM answered with SQL/DS in 1981 and **Database 2 (DB2)** for the MVS mainframe operating system in 1983. Db2 became a cornerstone of enterprise computing, and remains widespread in banking, insurance, and other industries where mainframes still process a large share of the world's transactions.
